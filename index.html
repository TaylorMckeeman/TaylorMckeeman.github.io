About Me

I'm an MSc student at the University of Calgary in Alberta, Canada. My current research is focused on the use of Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) based remote sensing for earlier detection of mountain pine beetle infected trees. My side interests in research include computational geography, UAVs in environmental applications, image processing and analysis, geographic information systems (GIS), and photogrammetry. Otherwise, I fill my spare time with programming, robotics, sports, and various outdoor activities.

Feel free to have a look around! My Projects and Photos pages can show you what I'm up to and what's new, and you can find various contact info at the bottom.
